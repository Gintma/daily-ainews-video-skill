# Research Rules

Use this reference when selecting and verifying daily news stories.

## Selection

- Default window: past 24 hours.
- Select stories that are recent, high-impact, and explainable in one short
  narration segment.
- Prefer stories with recognizable actors, real numbers, product launches,
  funding amounts, policy shifts, safety issues, benchmark claims, or platform
  changes.
- Avoid duplicate angles. If several sources report the same announcement, keep
  the primary source and use media coverage only as a popularity signal.

## Source Priority

1. Official blogs, newsroom posts, developer docs, regulatory filings, papers.
2. High-quality reporting from established outlets.
3. Roundups, newsletters, social posts, and forums only for discovery.

## Verification

For every story, capture:

- `title_en`
- `title_zh`
- `source`
- `url`
- `date`
- `metric`
- `translation`
- `callout`
- `script`

Do not include a story if the date or source URL cannot be verified.

## Writing Style

- Chinese, spoken, direct.
- One idea per sentence.
- Explain why it matters to builders, product people, investors, and AI users.
- Keep each story script short enough for its scene duration.
